Privacy

Short version: Arasul doesn't have our servers in the loop. Long version follows.

What stays on the drive

Everything that matters:

  • Your notes and project files — in content/, readable plaintext.
  • Your Claude OAuth token — in .boot/vault.enc, Argon2id + XChaCha20-Poly1305 encrypted with your passphrase.
  • Your chat history with Claude — in content/communication/ on the drive, never in our cloud.
  • Your project registry, configuration, memory — in memory/, readable plaintext.

What we collect

By default: nothing. Arasul makes no network calls unless you:

  • Click "Sign in with Anthropic" — your browser talks to anthropic.com, we're not in the loop.
  • Ask Claude a question — your message goes to anthropic.com directly from the drive; their privacy policy applies.
  • Check for updates — our updater hits arasul.dev/releases/feed.json with no identifying info.

You can optionally enable telemetry in Settings → Security. Off by default. What's in the payload: app version, OS, a random installation ID, event names (like "app_launch", "vault_unlock"), and duration-relative timestamps. Never: file contents, file names, chat messages, passphrases, wall-clock time, your identity.

What happens when you eject

The host computer forgets. No residue in temp directories (we clear per-session tmpdirs on eject), no cached tokens (they live in the vault, which is re-locked on eject), no history entries in the user's shell.

What happens if you lose the drive

Whoever finds it sees vault.enc — an opaque encrypted blob. Without your passphrase, extracting the Claude token requires brute-forcing Argon2id, which is designed to be too expensive to do at scale. Your notes and projects remain readable to anyone who has the drive — if you store sensitive material outside the vault, consider encrypting those individual files too.

What about the beta signup?

The form on signup.html doesn't POST anywhere — it composes a mailto: link that opens your email client with a pre-filled message to hello@arasul.dev. Your email lands in our normal inbox, which is also the only place it's stored. We reply once, then archive the thread. Ask us to forget and the thread gets deleted. No database, no CRM.
